Sea Turtles help maintain the health of the world’s oceans

For more than 100 million years, sea turtles have played a critical role in maintaining the health and balance of our marine habitats. They ensure productive coral reefs and transfer vital nutrients from the ocean to coastal dunes.

Throughout the last two centuries, harmful human activities have caused major disturbances in the ocean that have completely wiped sea turtles from some parts of the globe and pushed them to the brink of extinction.

What is a turtle hatchery?

Facilities specially designed for incubating and incubating sea turtle eggs They are generally run by conservation groups, government agencies, or individuals dedicated to the protection and conservation of turtle populations.

The Process of Turtle Hatcheries:

Turtle hatcheries typically begin by collecting turtle eggs from the beach or the nesting site and transferring them to the hatchery. The eggs are then placed in a protected area, such as an enclosure or a dug-out pit, and are monitored regularly to ensure that they are incubating properly. Once the eggs hatch, the baby turtles are carefully collected and released into the ocean or a safe area on the beach.

Why are Turtle Hatcheries Important?

Turtle hatcheries play a vital role in conserving and protecting sea turtles in Sri Lanka. These hatcheries provide a safe place for turtle eggs to hatch and release baby turtles into the sea, protecting them from natural predators and human activities. Sea turtles are an important part of the marine ecosystem and contribute to the balance of the ocean’s food chain. Additionally, turtle hatcheries support the local economy through ecotourism, providing jobs and income to communities.
